💸 Cost Barriers

Cost barriers refer to the high price of digital devices, internet services, and software that prevent many people from accessing technology. In many low-income communities, families cannot afford smartphones, laptops, or stable internet connections.

When technology is expensive, only certain groups benefit from digital growth, increasing the gap between the rich and the poor. The digital economy grows rapidly, but those who cannot afford entry are left further behind.

📉 Key Impacts

  • Limited access to online education and e-learning platforms
  • Reduced job and freelance opportunities
  • Unequal participation in the digital economy
  • Slower skill development for disadvantaged groups
  • Families forced to choose between internet and basic necessities

💰 The Hidden Costs

  • Data plans often exceed monthly budgets in developing nations
  • Device maintenance and repair costs are prohibitive
  • Software licensing fees for essential tools
  • Electricity costs for charging devices
  • Replacement costs for outdated technology

✅ Solutions We Support

  • Government subsidies for internet and devices
  • Affordable device programs with payment plans
  • Open-source software alternatives
  • Public access points in libraries and community centers
  • Zero-rating educational content
  • Community-owned internet infrastructure

Reducing cost barriers through affordable internet, subsidized devices, and public access points helps promote digital equality and supports SDG 10: Reduced Inequalities.
